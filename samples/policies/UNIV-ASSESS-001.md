## 4. Assessment validity and learning outcomes
Every summative assessment must generate observable evidence for the approved learning outcomes at the stated study level. The task, evidence required, rubric criteria, and mark allocation must be mutually consistent.

## 5. Mark allocation
The published rubric must total 100 percent or the approved maximum marks. All calculations and weightings must be independently checked before release.

## 6. Group assessment
Where group work contributes to an individual student's grade, the assessment design must include credible evidence of individual contribution and individual achievement. Acceptable evidence may include repository history, individual logs, peer evidence, demonstrations, or an individual oral defence. Peer ratings alone must not determine the grade.

## 7. Moderation decision
An automated system may assist with moderation analysis, but only the appointed academic moderator or authorised board may approve, reject, or require amendments to an assessment.
