## 2. Assignment-level declaration
Each assessment must state whether generative AI use is Closed, Guided, or Open. The statement must specify permitted uses, prohibited uses, disclosure requirements, verification duties, and the evidence students must retain.

## 3. Closed mode
Closed mode is appropriate where unaided individual performance is the intended construct and the conditions can be controlled. A blanket prohibition should not be used merely because the assessment design lacks observable process evidence.

## 4. Guided mode
Guided mode permits defined forms of assistance while preserving assessed student decisions. For programming work, permitted uses may include explaining errors, proposing test cases, or critiquing code. Students remain accountable for security, correctness, attribution, and oral explanation.

## 5. Open mode
Open mode may assess purposeful human–AI collaboration. Students must document significant model use, verify outputs, identify rejected suggestions, and defend the submitted artefact.

## 6. Equity
Where a specific commercial AI service is required, equivalent institutional access or an academically equivalent alternative must be provided.
